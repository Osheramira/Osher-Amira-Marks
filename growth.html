My Growth Journey

Reflecting on the stages of my intellectual and personal development throughout this course.

1

Critical Engagement

I came into this course with a "fixed mindset" about reading, considering texts as static facts to be memorized, not arguments to be analyzed. Rarely did I ever question the person behind the pen. This semester forced me to tear down that passive approach and to start actively questioning the text for perspective and bias. I learned that the most important question isn't just "what" the author wrote, but "why" they wrote it.

2

Voice Development

I began this journey paralyzed in my thoughts by the need to sound "perfect" and scholarly. All too often, my real thoughts were buried beneath heavy academic phrasing. In embracing the process of writing- the messy first drafts- I stripped away pretension. I found that a powerful academic voice is not derived from the usage of the most complicated vocabulary; rather, it's stating a unique perspective with clarity, forthrightness, and confidence.

3

Intellectual Humility

Through this, I came to realize that the strongest writers are not those who proclaim to know everything but those who question what they think they already know. I went from considering feedback as criticism to recognizing it as a necessary tool for refinement. Embracing uncertainty and seeking gaps in my logic allowed me to turn revision from a chore into a method through which to create work that is honest, nuanced, and intellectually sound.

4

Self-Efficacy

I developed the confidence to tackle ambiguity without hesitation. Whereas difficult texts or broad prompts once overwhelmed me, I now have the strategies with which to navigate them. I learned to replace trepidation with forward motion, using a personal toolkit of methods for analysis that can deconstruct complex ideas and build original, meaningful responses from the ground up.
