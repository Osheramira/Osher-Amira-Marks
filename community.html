Tu Beshvat

Tu Bishvat (the 15th of Shevat) is the "New Year for Trees." Originally an agricultural deadline, it is now celebrated as a holiday honoring nature and our connection to the land of Israel. Traditions include planting trees and eating the "Seven Species" of fruits (like figs, dates, and pomegranates) to celebrate growth and environmental stewardship.

Purim

Purim is a happy and major Jewish celebration held on the 14th day of Adar in commemoration of the salvation of the Jews from the wrath of Haman, just like in the Book of Esther. The major traditions include reading the Megillah, wearing costumes, consuming Hamantaschen, and giving to charity.

Mimouna

Mimouna is a festive Moroccan Jewish holiday celebrated immediately after Passover to mark the return of leavened bread, characterized by open-door hospitality and eating sweet treats like moufleta.

Zikaron Basalon- Holocaust Memorial Day

Zikaron BaSalon is a grass roots event that observes the commemoration of the memory of the Holocaust through private family gatherings in the home rather than in great venues. The name itself, "Memory in the Living Room," is significant in that it enables audiences to hear the stories of the survivors as well as express themselves in the arts.

Israel's Memorial Day

On October 7, 2023, Hamas-led militants launched a massive, coordinated surprise attack on Israel from the Gaza Strip. The offensive involved the firing of thousands of rockets and a ground invasion where militants breached the border fence, infiltrating Israeli military bases and civilian communities. The attack resulted in the deaths of approximately 1,200 people, mostly civilians, and the abduction of around 250 hostages into Gaza. Shavout

Shavuot is a major Jewish holiday celebrated seven weeks after Passover. It commemorates the giving of the Torah to the Jewish people at Mount Sinai and also marks the ancient wheat harvest. Traditions include staying up all night to study Torah (Tikkun Leil Shavuot), reading the Book of Ruth, decorating homes with greenery, and eating dairy foods like cheesecake and blintzes.

4th of July

The 4th of July (Independence Day) is the national holiday of the United States commemorating the adoption of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. It marks the birth of the nation and its separation from British rule. The day is celebrated with patriotic displays, fireworks, parades, barbecues, and family gatherings, often featuring the colors red, white, and blue.

Rosh Hashana

Rosh Hashana is the Jewish New Year, marking the beginning of the High Holy Days and the "Ten Days of Repentance." It is celebrated as the anniversary of the creation of the world (specifically Adam and Eve). The holiday is a time for introspection, prayer, and hearing the blast of the Shofar (ram's horn), which calls people to wake up and repent. It is customary to eat apples dipped in honey to symbolize the wish for a sweet new year.
